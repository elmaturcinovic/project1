Bosnia and Herzegovina

Yellow Fortress

The Yellow Fortress or The Yellow Bastion is part of the old walls which protected the city, built in the 18th century and it is part of the history of Sarajevo. It’s also called Top, because from that place during the Ramadan cannon shots mark the start of Iftar.

Mostar

Mostar is a small medieval town in central Herzegovina, located on the banks of the Neretva River. It was always the cultural and economic center of Herzegovina region.

Mostar

The Ottoman-style Old Bridge (Stari Most) from 16th century is the most notable architectural landmark of Mostar. Stretching 28 metres across the Neretva River, it connected the two sides of the city for 427 years, until it was destroyed in 1993 during the Bosnian War. Thanks to post-war restoration efforts, the bridge was reconstructed in 2004. Today, the Stari Most is Mostar’s (and probably Bosnia and Herzegovina’s) most famous sight.

Mostar

The Ottomans brought copperware into fashion in Bosnia. For centuries, artisans designed and crafted objects into plates, coffee pots and ornaments. Walk through Mostar’s cobbled streets leading up the Stari Most and listen to the clank, clank, clank of the hammer. Admire their designs and craftsmanship or hunt for a special Bosnian souvenir to take home.

Bjelašnica

Bjelašnica is a mountain in central Bosnia and Herzegovina. It is found directly to the southwest of Sarajevo, bordering Mt. Igman. Bjelašnica's tallest peak, by which the whole mountain group got its name, rises to an elevation of 2067 meters (6782 feet).

Bjelašnica

Mountain Bjelašnica is one of the mountains that hosted the Winter Olympic Games in 1984. This was not a surprise since some of its trails belong to the top European ski slopes. Also, its challenging forest runs are ideal for skilled skiers and snowboarders.

Nula lake

Lake “Nula” in Vares is the largest well of drinking water in BiH, and lovers of nature are trying to make an attractive tourist destination, in order for it not to be just a place for local population to swim during the summer season. This lake was created on the surface burrow of the former iron ore mine. It is about 500 metres long, 300 m wide and 100 m deep.People are visiting it not just for swimming, but also for fishing, walking, taking photos etc.

Sand Pyramids od Foča

On the ninth kilometer of the old Foča-Miljevina-Sarajevo road, in the place called Pirni Do, there are Sand Pyramids, and are a real masterpiece of the nature.This unique geomorphologic phenomenon has been created for many years. By erosion of the soil and under the influence of aggressive friction of frosts, summer heat, rain and strong winds, figures of interesting shapes were created. Their shape and composition are reminiscent of the Pyramids in Colorado (USA), Dolomites (Italy), the Valcea region in Romania, or the pyramids in Devil's Town (Đavolja Varoš, Serbia)

Kravica waterfalls

In Southern Bosnia, about 40km south of gorgeous Mostar on the Trebizat river lie the Kravica Waterfalls. The waterfalls themselves are about 25m high (80 feet)are 25 meters high and are so spectacular that they are also called “mini-Niagara falls”.

Neum

Neum is the only seaside tourist town in Bosnia and Herzegovina, built on the steep slopes of the gulf of Neum – Klek, which friendly opens its heart and gives its Mediterranean soul, welcoming each and every benevolent guest no matter what part of the world he is coming from. The Neum riviera is 9 km long, and thanks to the indentedness of the coast that length spreads out to 24 km.

Skakavac

Skakavac ("grasshopper" in English) is a waterfall in Bosnia and Herzegovina. It is 12 kilometres northeast of Sarajevo, above the village of Nahorevo. At 98 metres (322 ft), it is one of the tallest waterfalls in the Balkans. Visitors may hike from Sarajevo through Nahorevo and the Skakavac Nature Preserve and it is well known hike tour.
